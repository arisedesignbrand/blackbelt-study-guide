Martial Art Styles

🥋

Karate

Meaning "empty hands." Self-defense and sport using hand/foot techniques. Features linear punches, circular strikes, multiple kicking techniques, kata, and sparring. Includes natural and tension stances.

🇯🇵 Japan / Okinawa
🦵

Tae Kwon Do / Tang Soo Do

TKD: "Art of kicking and punching." Largest martial art in the world. Olympic sport. Known for flying/jump kicks. Founded April 11, 1955 by General Choi Hong Hi.

Tang Soo Do: "Art of the knife hand." Founded by Grandmaster Hwang Kee. Chuck Norris is its most famous US practitioner.

🇰🇷 Korea
💪

Muay Thai / Kickboxing

"Art of eight limbs." Uses knees and elbows. Developed in 1650 by King Naresuen. Modern rules set by King Rama VII in 1920s. Kru Mark (Deth Ko Sin) is Freestyle's Muay Thai connection.

🇹🇭 Thailand
🤼

Judo / BJJ

Judo: "Gentle way." Created 1882 by Jigoro Kano. Olympic sport. Throws, pins, joint locks. Three phases: Kuzushi, Tsukuri, Kake.

BJJ: Founded by Helio Gracie. Ground grappling, throws using leverage. Mitsubishi Maedo brought judo/jiu-jitsu to Brazil in 1910.

🇯🇵/🇧🇷 Japan & Brazil
🛡️

Krav Maga / Haganah

Krav Maga: Offensive Israeli military art. Developed by Imi Lichenfield in 1940s. No ranking system. Official self-defense of Israeli Defense Forces.

Haganah: Jewish paramilitary organization founded 1920. Core of the Israel Defense Forces.

🇮🇱 Israel
🪃

Escrima / Arnis-Kali

National sport of the Philippines. Weapon-based fighting: sticks, knives, bladed weapons. Close-range strikes and throws. Practitioners train to reduce blinking. Belt ranks 1–10. Dan Inosanto is an important CA practitioner.

🇵🇭 Philippines
🐉

Wushu / Kung Fu / Wing Chun

Kung Fu: "Skill/ability." Many systems. Energetic kicking/punching or subtle techniques. Influenced by Buddhism, Taoism, Confucianism.

Wing Chun: Most influential Chinese art. Close-range linear strikes, simultaneous attack/defense. Bruce Lee began here.

🇨🇳 China
✊

Kempo / Kajukenbo

Kempo: First Americanized martial art. Focuses on strikes, chops, punches, kicks. Introduced to Hawaii by James Mitose. Updated by Ed Parker.

Kajukenbo: Hybrid of Karate, Judo, Ju-jitsu, Kenpo, Chinese boxing. Developed 1947 by the "Black Belt Society." Goal: invincible on the street.

🇯🇵/🇺🇸 Japan/Hawaii
🌊

Aikido / Ninjutsu

Aikido: Developed by Morihei Ueshiba in 1942. Joint locks and throws. "Way of unifying with life energy." Non-violent philosophy.

Ninjutsu: Feudal Japanese art. Espionage, commando warfare. Outlawed 17th century. Stephen Hayes is a famous modern practitioner.

🇯🇵 Japan
⚡

Jeet Kune Do

"Way of the intercepting fist." Eclectic hybrid style founded by Bruce Lee in 1967. Meant to free practitioners from one style. Resembles Wing Chun, Arnis, TKD, Wrestling, Thai boxing. Key instructors: Dan Inosanto, Dan Lee, Ted Wong.

🇺🇸 USA (Bruce Lee)
🥊

Wrestling / Boxing

Boxing: First documented match 1681 in Britain. Jack Broughton created first rules in 1743. Modern rules by John Sholto Douglas in 1865. Olympic sport since 1904. Sanctioned bodies: WBC, IBF, WBA.

Three minute rounds, approved gloves.

🌍 Global
☯️

Tai Chi / Yoga

Tai Chi: "Meditation in motion." Mind-body practice from China as a martial art. Low-impact, slow motion. Promotes flow of Qi (life energy) and Yin-Yang balance. Movements named for animal actions.

🇨🇳 China
🤸

XMA / Tricking

Extreme Martial Arts — combines traditional martial arts with gymnastics, acrobatics, and freestyle moves. Team Freestyle Performance is Sensei Jackson's performance team.

🇺🇸 USA (Modern)
🌐

Other Styles

Sambo: Russian jacket wrestling, 1930s. 20+ wrestling styles. First sport in 1968.

Savate: French kickboxing, 16th century. Sailors in Marseilles.

Capoeira: Brazilian — kicks, flips, dance. Founded by African slaves 300+ years ago.

Silat: Indonesian, ~1000 years old. National defense sport of Indonesia.

Sumo: Japanese combat. Force opponent out of 15-foot ring or make them touch the ground.

🌍 Various

Important Organizations

Organization

CDT — Compliance Direction & Takedown

Created by Tom Patiri in 1992. Designed for real-world situations to physically control/disarm a hostile aggressor. Originally for Law Enforcement; now used in hospitals, schools, airlines, religious organizations. Sensei K is a CDT Tactical Master Instructor.

Organization

UFC — Ultimate Fighting Championship

Founded November 1993. Based in Las Vegas. 40+ events per year. Championships are 5 rounds × 5 minutes. Scored by 3 judges on a 10-point system. 31 types of fouls. Victories by submission, KO, TKO, or decision (unanimous, split, majority).

Organization

WTF — World Taekwondo Federation

International governing body for Taekwondo. Member of the Association of Summer Olympic International Federations. Renamed from "WTF" in June 2017.

Organization

IBJJF — International Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu Federation

Hosts the world's biggest BJJ tournaments. Manages ranking points across multiple events. Points calculated by placement × tournament ranking × season year.

Organization

Kodokan

"A place for the study of the way." Headquarters of worldwide Judo. Founded 1882 by Jigoro Kano. Eight-story building in Tokyo. Issues ranks; many judoka register with Kodokan. Vladimir Putin is a registered judoka.

Founder of JKD

Bruce Lee

1940–1973 | Born San Francisco, raised Hong Kong. Began Wing Chun at 13. Founded Jeet Kune Do in 1967. Started as Kato in The Green Hornet (1966). Famous for one-inch punch and one-finger push-ups. Died of cerebral edema (brain swelled 13%) at age 32. Named by Time as one of 100 most influential people of the 20th century.

Tang Soo Do

Chuck Norris

Studied Tang Soo Do. Discharged from US Air Force. Fighting career 1964–1974. Famously fought Bruce Lee in Enter the Dragon / Way of the Dragon. Defeated multiple times in tournament by Joe Lewis. Most famous US practitioner of Tang Soo Do.

🎬
Stunt Legend

Jackie Chan

Born April 7, 1954 — Hong Kong. Had minor roles in two Bruce Lee films. Breakthrough with Drunken Master. Known for performing most of his own stunts. Top grossing films include Rush Hour, Kung Fu Panda (voice of Monkey), and The Forbidden Kingdom.

🏆
Wushu Champion

Jet Li

Born 1963 — Beijing, China. Started Wushu at age 8. National Wushu Champion at age 11 (held 5 years). Film debut in Shaolin Temple (1982). Moved to Hong Kong then Hollywood. Appeared with Jackie Chan in The Forbidden Kingdom.

🥊
Boxing Legend

Muhammad Ali

Born January 17, 1942 — Died 2016. 1960 Olympic Gold Medal (Rome). Retired 1981. Changed name from Cassius Clay. Famous for opposition to Vietnam draft (stripped of passport, banned 3 years). Received MLK Award 1970. "Float like a butterfly, sting like a bee." Bruce Lee was inspired by his footwork. Developed Parkinson's later in life.

🥋
Kickboxing Pioneer

Joe Lewis

1944–2012 — Born North Carolina. Retired undefeated in heavyweight kickboxing. Defeated Chuck Norris multiple times in tournament. Trained with Bruce Lee 1967–1968. "Father of Kickboxing" in the western world. Signature move: fast left leg sidekick. Paved the way for modern MMA. Passed away from brain cancer 2012.

🤼
BJJ Founders

The Gracie Family

From Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. Founders of Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu. Helio Gracie (1913–2009) still practicing at age 90. Carlos Gracie co-founded the art. Rorion Gracie was one of 5 founders of UFC (1993). Brought BJJ to the US in the 1980s. UFC gave BJJ credibility globally.

🥊
Boxing

Floyd Mayweather

Born February 24, 1977. Competed 1996–2007 and 2009–2015. Retired with a perfect record of 50–0. Considered one of the greatest defensive boxers in history.

🏅
MMA / Judo

Ronda Rousey

Born February 1, 1987 — Riverside, CA. First American woman to medal in Olympic Judo. First female fighter in UFC Hall of Fame. UFC debut 2015, WWE 2018. Her mother was a decorated Judoka. Struggled to speak until age 6 — required intensive speech therapy. Retired from Judo at 21; began MMA at 22.

🎓
TKD Pioneer

Jhoon Rhee

Born January 7, 1932 — Korea. "Father of American Taekwondo." Arrived in the US in the 1950s and introduced TKD to America. Met Bruce Lee and Ed Parker at the 1964 Long Beach International Karate Championships.

🥇
Kenpo Founder

Ed Parker

Founder of American Kenpo. Organized the first Long Beach International Karate Championships where Bruce Lee, Jhoon Rhee, and Ed Parker met. Revised Kenpo techniques into a logical progression of basics, self-defense, and freestyle.

🥷
Ninjutsu Expert

Stephen Hayes

Traveled to Japan in 1970s. Trained under Masaaki Hatsumi (34th Grandmaster of Ninjutsu, founder of Bujinkan). 1985: Black Belt Hall of Fame — Instructor of the Year. 1993: Judan (10th-degree black belt) in Ninjutsu. 1997: Founded To-Shin Do. 1990s: Served as the Dalai Lama's personal protection escort.

Concept of the Belts

Each belt represents a stage of growth in mind, body, and spirit. The journey from white belt to black belt is not just physical — it reflects personal development, discipline, and character. Achieving Black Belt Excellence means doing your personal best in all aspects of life.

Key Terms & Definitions

Do (道)

Pronounced "doe." Japanese word meaning "The Way" or "The Journey." Used in art names: Judo (gentle way), Taekwondo (way of foot and fist), Aikido (way of unifying with life energy).

Jitsu / Jutsu (術)

Meaning "art" or "technique." Used in names like: Ju-Jitsu (art of flexibility), Ninjutsu (ninja art).

Dan / Degree

These mean the same thing — both refer to the rank/level of a black belt. (e.g., 3rd Dan = 3rd Degree Black Belt)

Judoka

A person who practices or is an expert in Judo.

Kata

Formal practice sequences / forms. A set pattern of movements used for practice and demonstration.

Randori

Free exercise / sparring in Judo. As opposed to formal kata practice.

Kuzushi / Tsukuri / Kake

The three phases of Judo: Kuzushi = unbalancing the opponent; Tsukuri = positioning into a dominant pose; Kake = execution of the throw or technique.

Qi (Chi / Ki)

An energy force thought to flow through the body. Tai Chi and Aikido promote the flow and balance of Qi. Yin and Yang — opposing elements kept in harmony.

Dojo

A martial arts training hall or school. Literally means "place of the way."

Sensei

Japanese term meaning "teacher" or "one who has come before." Used as a title of respect for a martial arts instructor.
